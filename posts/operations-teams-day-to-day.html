Operations Teams: Day-to-Day

April 14, 2018 | Category: Operations

Magicians practice their craft for years. They continouly hone their skills so their tricks do truly look like magic. Audiences watch in wonder and begin to question wether magic does really exist. This is what Operations or DevOps is, magic. Well, not really magic but some seem to think what Ops teams do is black magic. They believe we sold our soul to Linus Torvalds to gain some dark arts power. In reality we are no different than a software engineer or a system administrator. We have just learned to meld the two fields to provide solutions to engineering teams so they ship code efficiently and consistently.

I get asked a lot by engineers, developers, or people wanting a career change what is involved in DevOps. What do you do? The answer is not always straight forward. A lot of what we do depends on the culture of where we work. If the company culture is very corporate, we might only work on builds/releases and that's all. If the culture is more open, then we might do more that builds and releases. We might create internal tools for the engineers to use in their daily work. It just depends.

Fear not! There are some common tasks that Operations Engineers handle. That is the point of this article. I want to cover those areas so people who are thinking of making the transistion into Ops will have a decent understanding of what your day will look like.
